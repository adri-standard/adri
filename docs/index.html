An Open Standard for Data Readiness

ADRI is a scoring system for data sources that measures how well they communicate quality signals to AI agents. Just like nutrition labels tell you what's in your food, ADRI tells you what quality signals exist in your data.

Why We Created ADRI

As AI agents become more prevalent, we noticed a critical gap: no standard way to evaluate whether data sources provide the signals agents need to make reliable decisions.

ADRI fills this gap with transparent, consistent metrics that anyone can apply to any dataset, creating a common language for data quality that benefits the entire AI community.

ADRI Dimensions

  • Freshness: Does the data tell agents when it was created or updated? Without this signal, your agent might recommend a product that's been out of stock for weeks.
  • Validity: Does the data include signals about its structural correctness? Without this signal, your agent might make calculations with corrupted numbers.
  • Completeness: Can agents distinguish between "missing" and "not applicable"? Without this signal, your agent can't tell if a blank field means "no data" or "doesn't apply."
  • Consistency: Are related data points compatible with each other? Without this signal, your agent might use prices from one currency with quantities from another.
  • Plausibility: Can agents detect outliers or implausible values? Without this signal, your agent can't tell when a number is suspiciously high or low.

ADRI is completely open-source. We believe data readiness is too important to be proprietary.

Addressing Data Readiness Issues

Once you've identified data readiness issues, here are approaches to address them:

DIY Approach

Build custom data pipelines that add metadata like timestamps, validity checks, and completeness signals.

Best for: Teams with dedicated data engineering resources and simpler agent requirements.

Challenges: Maintenance overhead, consistency across sources, and scalability.

Middleware Solution

Implement a data quality layer between your agents and data sources to inject the necessary signals.

Best for: Teams with multiple agents accessing the same data sources.

Challenges: Integration complexity and potential performance impacts.

Agent-Integrated Approach

Incorporate data quality checking directly into your agent's reasoning processes.

Best for: Specialized agents with domain-specific quality requirements.

Challenges: Redundant implementation across agents and prompt engineering complexity.
